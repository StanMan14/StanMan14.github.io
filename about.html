About the founder:

As Head Boy of Eden Christian School in 2023, he balanced leadership duties with a growing passion for visual art, earning schoolwide recognition for both his talent and his commitment to community creativity.



In late 2023, Stanley entered Die Pos’s inaugural “Die Pos Palet: van pen tot kwas” art competition, submitting one of 185 entries from learners across Bela-Bela, Mokopane and beyond. His mixed-media piece—executed in both ink pen and brush—clinched the overall trophy, marking him as the competition’s standout artist.

Odette Graskie, the Johannesburg-based judge and MA graduate in visual arts, praised his work for “very expressive mark-making,” a “brilliant interpretation of the theme,” and “technique with different textures and patterns.”



Beyond the competition circuit, Stanley’s portfolio extends to portraiture and environmental art. In a special end-of-year project, he drew self-portraits of each of his teachers—a series described by the school as showcasing “unbelievable technique and talent.”

His meticulous line-work captures personality and mood, while subtle brush accents bring warmth to each image.

Inside the halls of Eden Christian School, blank notice boards become canvases under Stanley’s hand. He routinely adorns them with hand-lettered inspirational quotes surrounded by intricate illustrations—flowers, geometric patterns and miniature scenes—that turn corridors into daily galleries of motivation.

True to the competition’s “pen to brush” challenge, Stanley fluidly shifts between fine-line ink work and expressive paint strokes. Cross-hatching, stippling and bold swaths of color converge to form rich textures and dynamic compositions—whether he’s composing figurative portraits or exploring abstract shapes.

His work reveals an eye for balance and thematic depth, continually evolving to inspire his community and peers alike.
